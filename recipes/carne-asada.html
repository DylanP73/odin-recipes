Carne Asada "The Grilled Meat"

Ideally you need to marinade for at least 4 hours (overnight will be so much better).

First thing to do is choose the right cut of meat.

I have tried more expensive steaks, but it's just not necessary for this meal. The best cut is a cheap thin cut of Flank Steak, Frying Steak, Skirt Steak or just simply thin cut beef. I believe many of us have our own word for it.

I would use about 1.5kg (If there are 6 or so people eating).

Chop up the meat into small strips about 7cm by 1.5cm (or there about)

Add to a marinade bag or bowl with:

Ingredients

  • Half a cup (a Good handful) of Fresh Coriander very finely chopped
  • 3 Tablespoons of Olive Oil
  • 2 Tablespoons Brown Sugar
  • Quarter of a cup of Soy Sauce
  • 1 Orange (Juiced)
  • 1 Lime (Juiced)
  • 4 Cloves of Garlic (Minced or Finely chopped)
  • 1 Small Jalape√±o Pepper (Finely Chopped)
  • 1 Teaspoon of Ground Cumin
  • A good pinch of Salt and Pepper (I go for a full teaspoon)

Preparation

Add everything together with the thinly cut steak - Simply toss it, mix it or shake it in a bag.

Place it in the fridge until you are ready to cook it (Ideally for the next day)

  • I do like to visit the fridge and flip it the other way quite a lot

Cooking Methods

I cook in either a wok in the kitchen or a Skillet on my BBQ (I find the wok easier as you can use the sides for everything to tumble around).

  1. You can now cook this to your liking, personally I like it just about done like a medium rare. It's also great if you overdo it a little with the burnt ends
  2. Leave it to rest a few minutes and lightly chop in to it with a knife on a chopping board to create smaller more shredded chunks.
    • (This isn't essential, it just stops people taking so much when they scoop it out for their tacos).

Serving

Add to a serving bowl and drop in a large spoon or kitchen tongs for everyone to serve themselves.

This now accompanies everything else you will have created for your Mexican meal

  • Flour Tortillas
  • Guacamole
  • Pico De Gallo

Play around with it and make it your own: I often change this one up a little with a big dollop of honey, sticky teriyaki sauce or whatever takes my fancy at the time.

.Sometimes, I have just cooked the meat on the BBQ as one whole marinated piece and chopped it up later. Although this works a bit better when you are actually having a BBQ rather than a few guests over in the dining room.
